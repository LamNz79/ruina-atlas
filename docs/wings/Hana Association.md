# &#x20;                   **Hana Association**

&#x20;                                 Head of the Associations, overseeing the system of Fixers and Offices



#### Trivia:

* The Hana Association is named after the Korean word for the number one.



* This is reflected in their logo, with either the Arabic numeral 1 or the Roman numeral I being placed in the center of the design.



* This appears to line up with Yi Sang's Korean origins and placement as Limbus Company's first Sinner.



* Prominently featured in the Hana Association are the four cardinal \[trigrams]; ☰ (qián), ☱ (duì), ☲ (lí), and ☳ (zhèn).



* The presence of these trigrams within their logo appears to be directly inspired by their presence within \[South Korea's flag], both being placed in identical locations within the design and depicted in black.



* These trigrams are explicitly referenced within the name of one of the Hana Association's Combat Pages - True Trigram Formation - and the Four Trigrams passives within their Key Pages.



* These trigrams appear on the standard Hana Association South and North Section uniforms, as seen during the Reception of Hana Association and Canto VII: The Dream Ending respectively.



#### General:

* The Hana Association handles the administration of all other Associations and Fixer Offices. They're responsible for issuing licenses,\[1] grading Fixers and their Offices based on their efforts, assigning them Grades from 1-9, as well as the Color status. The Hana Association also has the authority to assess threat levels of Syndicates and Phenomenon's appearing in the City.



* Their equipment consists of trigrams, which can morph into whatever shape they deem necessary in combat.



* When arriving at District 16, the Sinners take up an Open Contract that the Hana Association had posted. With Han-ul as the representative, the Hana Association handled the broader organization of the operation regarding the eradication of La Manchaland.



#### Classifications:

* The Hana Association uses multiple classification systems to grade various social structures found in the City.



* To become a Fixer, one has to acquire a license from the Hana Association. Once acquired, a Fixer receives their Grade, starting out at 9 and being able to climb as far as Grade 1. Fixers that prove themselves exceptionally skilled receive a Color to distinguish them from regular Fixers. Being granted the Color status is non-negotiable. The Hana Association also revokes status and is capable of demoting Fixers should they go against the rules provided by the Hana Association.



* Phenomena, Syndicates and anything that could prove dangerous to the public safety is categorized based on the Association's rating system. These categories are as follows in ascending order:

&#x09;Urban Myth

&#x09;Urban Legend

&#x09;Urban Plague

&#x09;Urban Nightmare

&#x09;Star of the City



* According to Walter, categorization is based on how much people are willing to pay to eliminate the threat. The body count of victims have previously also factored into the grading.\[2] Threats that prove themselves to be severe enough to defy even the Star of the City categorization can be labeled as Impurity and will be handled directly by The Head. Impurities can only be labeled by the latter and handling them is no longer within the authority of the Hana Association.
